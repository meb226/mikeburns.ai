Layer 01

The Knowledge Framework

Not a list of tactics. A structured understanding of how advocacy outcomes actually unfold, drawn from the peer-reviewed research and hard-won practitioner experience that most AI systems have never seen.

Consolidated from Real Expertise

We distilled 50 strategic principles from peer-reviewed research on lobbying effectiveness and interviews with senior practitioners. This isn't a list of tactics; it's a decision framework that understands context.

The principles encode hard-won lessons: why 60% of lobbying campaigns fail to change policy, how defenders hold structural advantages, when coalition diversity matters more than size, and why resources alone explain less than 5% of success.

Each principle includes conditional logic. Status quo defense requires different positioning than offensive campaigns. High-salience issues demand different tool selection than quiet regulatory fights. The framework adapts recommendations to your prospect's specific situation.

Most importantly, these principles stay invisible in your output. The memo doesn't cite research or drop academic jargon. It applies the insights naturally, the way a seasoned BD partner would: informed by deep knowledge, but speaking the language of your prospect.

The framework draws from foundational work on legislative subsidy theory, policy window dynamics, and coalition formation research. It incorporates practitioner insights on venue shopping, competitive response patterns, and the counterintuitive finding that money matters far less than strategic positioning.

The difference: Generic AI summarizes what lobbying is. PitchSource knows when to recommend grasstops mobilization versus quiet inside-game engagement, when to emphasize coalition breadth versus depth, and how to position defensive versus offensive campaigns.
